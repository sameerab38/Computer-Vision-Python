Comparison of Example 1 and Example 2:

The first example is cropped from the cheekbone and hair border point of the women in the picture. The second example is cropped at one of the moles on her cheek. As we can see:

  • The average intensity of the first example is very less than the average intensity of example 2. This is because the example 1 is less homogeneous than example 2.
  • The standard deviations of b,g,r channels of example 1 are higher than b,g,r channels of example 2 showing that the colors are inhomogeneous in example 1 when compared to example 2.
  • The individual color histograms of these two examples also tell the same. There are single peaks in each of the color histograms in example 2 showing that there is one dominant color.
